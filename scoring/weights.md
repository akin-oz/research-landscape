# Default weights

The baseline below is a starting profile for a research-focused applicant, not a ranking formula. Weights sum to 100 and may be replaced by applicant-declared priorities. Missing dimensions are excluded from the denominator and prominently disclosed.

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Research alignment | 20 | Direct fit strongly shapes supervision and project opportunity. |
| Mentorship | 15 | Support structures materially affect graduate experience. |
| Research quality | 12 | Evidence of rigorous, field-aware research practice matters. |
| Publication activity | 10 | Activity signals opportunity but is not quality alone. |
| Open science | 8 | Transparency and access benefit reproducibility. |
| Software engineering | 7 | Important for software-producing research environments. |
| Scientific computing | 7 | Important for computational research capability. |
| Student outcomes | 6 | Aggregate, contextual outcomes can inform expectations. |
| Funding activity | 5 | Supports feasibility; availability is field-dependent. |
| International collaboration | 4 | May broaden networks and research exposure. |
| Industry collaboration | 3 | Relevant for translational and applied paths. |
| Interdisciplinary work | 3 | Valuable for applicants seeking cross-field work. |

Any report that applies these weights must state why they fit its use case and include a sensitivity note for material alternative priorities.
