# Adem Tekin — due-diligence dossier

This is an evidence-first briefing for a prospective graduate student whose intended intersection is **scientific software engineering + computational materials science + AI-assisted materials discovery**. It is not an admission assessment, a ranking, or a claim about private lab practices.

## Bottom line

**Interpretation — high confidence:** Prof. Dr. Adem Tekin is an unusually direct academic match for a software engineer who wants software to be a first-class research object, not merely a support activity. The combination of current crystal-structure-prediction work, public Python implementations, ML force-field and materials-screening work, HPC access, and an active student/project pipeline is concrete rather than aspirational. The strongest immediate bridge is a rigorously engineered contribution around PNcsp+/CSP workflows.

That conclusion is conditional. The public record does **not** establish meeting cadence, authorship rules, code-maintainership rights, expectations for a software-heavy thesis, current student load, or alumni PhD placements. Those are the decision gates for an in-person meeting, not details to assume.

## Evidence rules

- **Fact** means a statement directly supported by the linked source.
- **Interpretation** is a reasoned applicant-specific conclusion; it names its confidence.
- **Unknown** is deliberately not converted into a positive or negative signal.
- **Confidence** describes the evidence quality and scope, not the quality of a person or lab.

The source hierarchy is: institutional record and primary paper/code first; self-maintained public profiles second; discovery/indexing pages only when a publisher page was inaccessible. Public sources are current through the date in the frontmatter and may change.
