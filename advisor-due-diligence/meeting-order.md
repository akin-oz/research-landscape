# Meeting order and decision gates

## Recommended outreach sequence

Send all three messages in the same week. Do not manufacture scarcity by waiting for one answer before contacting the next person. Schedule conversations in this order if choices are required.

| Priority | Advisor | Recommended format | Why this comes first | The meeting must establish |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Adem Tekin | In person if practical; otherwise a 30-minute video call. | This is the highest-evidence intersection of materials computation, ML/crystal prediction, and public research software. [Group](https://web.itu.edu.tr/~tekinad/) · [PNcsp+](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC13085239/) | An active MSc-sized question; a software component with intellectual ownership; code/data/authoring rules; capacity and funding. |
| 2 | Hande Toffoli | In person if practical; otherwise a 30-minute video call. | A group-maintained, date-sensitive public student cohort and an established DFT/MD programme make a live conversation unusually informative about research culture and project fit. [Group](https://www.compmatsci.com/) · [students](https://www.compmatsci.com/msc-students) | The current computational stack, project availability, expected theory depth, and whether reproducibility/software can be an assessed research output. |
| 3 | Sadettin Yavuz Uğurlu | Start with a short research-scoping call; visit after the project/co-supervision route is confirmed. | The direct software fit is high, but public evidence leaves more foundational questions open: materials project, thesis record, team, data rights, and supervision arrangement. [Profile](https://avesis.akdeniz.edu.tr/syavuzugurlu) · [materials code](https://github.com/yauz3/coating_motifs_ml) | Whether a current materials project exists, who co-supervises, what data can be used/released, and whether an MSc thesis is formally feasible. |

## Who to visit in person

**Visit Tekin first** if only one visit can happen immediately. The visit should test whether the attractive technical overlap becomes a concrete research contract: which project, what scientific question, what code base, what student ownership, and what publication/release pathway.

**Visit Toffoli as the second priority** because an in-person conversation can clarify the day-to-day research cadence, student interaction, and the boundary between physics/science work and workflow engineering in a group with a visible current cohort.

**Visit Uğurlu after a short call confirms the prerequisites.** This is not a judgment about quality. It prevents a costly meeting from being built on unverified assumptions about project availability, data rights, or co-supervision.

## One-page briefing to bring

Bring a single page, not a portfolio dump:

1. A three-line career transition statement: software architecture → scientific Python → computational materials research.
2. One selected research problem from the advisor's own work.
3. A two-part deliverable: a materials-science result and a versioned/reproducible software or data artifact.
4. Two concrete examples from Materials Atlas or Research Landscape that demonstrate provenance, source traceability, data contracts, testing, and documentation.
5. Three questions that require the advisor's judgment, not an admissions answer.

Avoid a general "AI for materials" proposal. The serious signal is a narrow research hypothesis plus an engineering artifact that makes the result reproducible.

## Meeting decision gates

Score no one from charisma or reputation. After each meeting, record direct answers to these gates.

| Gate | Green answer | Yellow / red answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Research question | A bounded, current question with methods and a plausible MSc timeline. | "We can decide later" with no problem or data source. |
| Ownership | Candidate owns a named model/workflow/data package and a scientific analysis. | Candidate is only asked to maintain others' scripts. |
| Supervision | Regular cadence, named domain guidance, and an accessible senior/peer research community. | Unclear primary advisor, unclear co-supervision, or no cadence. |
| Reproducibility | Versioned inputs/outputs, code access, and a realistic publication/release decision path. | Code/data are unavailable with no permitted independent contribution. |
| Career development | A plan for methods training, paper authorship, presentations, and references. | Only an undefined project outcome. |

Do not treat a missing public GitHub repository as a red flag by itself. Treat refusal or inability to define ownership and reproducibility expectations as a decision-relevant risk.

## Follow-up discipline

Within 24 hours, send a four-sentence thank-you that repeats the agreed research question, the candidate-owned artifact, one next action, and any requested material. Within seven days, send only the promised one-page note or small reproducible code sample—not a speculative full proposal.
