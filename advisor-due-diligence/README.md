# Advisor due diligence

This directory is a decision-preparation dossier for one declared applicant profile. It is not an institutional ranking, an admission prediction, or a claim about any advisor's private conduct.

## Candidate profile used for the assessment

- Senior software engineer with more than ten years of industry experience.
- Software architecture and open-source contribution experience.
- Building Materials Atlas and Research Landscape while learning Scientific Python.
- Long-term direction: **scientific software engineering + computational materials science + AI for materials discovery**.

## Evidence rules

Each dossier prioritizes official institutional/group pages, full scholarly publications, funder or thesis records, and public research repositories. Public sources can establish research outputs, methods, visible group composition, and publicly documented software practice. They usually cannot establish current intake capacity, day-to-day supervision style, unpublished projects, group dynamics, funding availability, or the candidate's chance of admission.

| Label | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Fact** | Directly stated or demonstrable in a linked source. |
| **Interpretation** | A reasoned assessment grounded in the cited facts; it is not presented as a fact. |
| **Unknown** | Not established by the reviewed public evidence. It is not a negative finding. |
| **High / medium / low confidence** | Confidence in the specific conclusion's public evidence, not a judgment of the person or group. |

Publication reviews explain methods, datasets or systems, and the research contribution where accessible. They do not restate abstracts, infer methods from a title, or treat a preprint/accepted manuscript as the same as a final published article.

## Decision discipline

The reports deliberately separate four questions that should be confirmed in a meeting:

1. Is there an active, fundable MSc-sized research question?
2. Can a software artifact be an explicit, credited part of the thesis rather than informal support work?
3. Who would supervise the materials science, code ownership, publication authorship, and IP/release decisions?
4. Does the advisor have capacity and interest for this particular applicant at the relevant intake?

No report substitutes for those answers. A positive recommendation means "worth a carefully prepared conversation now," not "guaranteed supervision" or "guaranteed international placement."
