## Recommendation

Pursue a **two-track first round**:

1. **Primary application target — Adem Tekin:** offer a narrow, materials-grounded proposal around crystal prediction, screening, or energy materials with a research-software deliverable that is necessary for the science.
2. **Primary scientific-depth alternative — Hande Toffoli:** offer a DFT/MD-centred project in which reproducibility, workflow engineering, and analysis are attached to a real atomistic hypothesis.
3. **High-upside exploratory route — Sadettin Yavuz Uğurlu:** pursue only if a conversation verifies a live materials question, formal supervision/co-supervision, data rights, and a credible MSc timeline.

## The non-negotiable condition

For every option, do **not** accept a thesis framed only as "improving software." The proposal must commit to both:

- a falsifiable materials-science question; and
- an owned, documented, testable software/data/workflow artifact whose role in producing or validating the result is explicit.

That framing turns the applicant’s industry experience from auxiliary support into research capability and produces portfolio evidence useful for future doctoral applications.

## Before any application

Get an affirmative answer to these five questions in writing or in a follow-up note:

1. What active project and scientific question could an MSc own?
2. What methods and data would be available at the beginning?
3. Can the candidate own a software/reproducibility deliverable and receive appropriate authorship/credit?
4. Who provides domain supervision, and how often would research meetings occur?
5. What would make the first six months successful?

If the answer to any of the first four is vague, preserve optionality rather than treating the advisor’s reputation as a substitute for a project plan.
