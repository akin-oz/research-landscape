# First-contact strategy

## Objective

Ask for research guidance and a short conversation—not admission, supervision, funding, or a recommendation. The message should make it easy for an advisor to assess intellectual fit without pressure.

## Shared structure

1. **One precise evidence hook** — a paper, public repository, course, or active project.
2. **One credible personal bridge** — senior software engineering, scientific Python transition, and a small relevant technical contribution.
3. **One research question** — framed around the advisor's work rather than a generic "AI" slogan.
4. **One low-friction request** — 20–30 minutes for advice about a research direction.
5. **One graceful exit** — no expectation of an application decision or a reply beyond what is convenient.

Do not attach a CV or a large product deck in the first note unless requested. Link to a small, polished public artifact only if it is genuinely relevant and maintainable.

## Introducing Materials Atlas and Research Landscape

Use them as evidence of working habits, not as brands that need endorsement.

> I have been building small open projects around traceable research metadata, reproducible documentation, and maintainable software. They have made me interested in how a computational-materials result can be packaged with its assumptions, inputs, and evidence—not only in producing a prediction.

Then immediately connect that habit to one source-backed research problem. Avoid asking the advisor to review either project in the first conversation.

## Tone and timing

- Keep the initial email to roughly 150–190 words.
- Use the advisor’s preferred/public professional name and a factual reference to their work.
- Send on a working day and offer a flexible time window.
- Contact all three within a week, but personalize each note fully.
- If there is no response, send one respectful follow-up after 10–14 days; do not use repeated reminders.

## What not to ask first

- "Can you accept me?"
- "Do you have funding?" before a research conversation establishes fit.
- "Can I work remotely?" before project and programme constraints are understood.
- "Can you guarantee a PhD abroad / a reference?"
- "Can I use your lab to build my product?"

Instead, ask how the advisor would scope a scientifically credible software-enabled materials question, what preparation they would advise, and whether a brief conversation would be useful.
