# Due diligence — Sadettin Yavuz Uğurlu

This is an evidence-first supervisory due-diligence dossier for a senior software
engineer moving toward scientific software engineering, computational materials
science, and AI for materials discovery. It is **not** an admissions assessment,
an institutional ranking, or a claim that a supervision place is available.

## Bottom line

**Conditional first-contact recommendation.** Dr. Öğr. Üyesi Sadettin Yavuz
Uğurlu is unusually well evidenced as a *computational research-software and
machine-learning* mentor: his public work includes reproducible Python pipelines
for docking, molecular-property learning, and a concrete materials bridge in
YbSi–mullite–Si environmental-barrier-coating hardness prediction. The evidence
does **not** yet establish a mature materials-informatics lab, public student
outcomes, a funded local research programme, available MSc capacity, or a
formal materials co-supervision arrangement. Those are decision gates, not
minor details. **Confidence: medium.**

The highest-value meeting outcome is therefore not an admission request. It is a
test of whether an MSc can be built around a bounded, publishable, openly
maintained materials-data/software contribution—ideally with Emre Bal or another
experimentally grounded materials co-supervisor.

## Evidence convention

- **Fact** means the linked source directly states or demonstrates it.
- **Interpretation** is a reasoned fit judgement from cited facts; it is not a
  promise about the advisor's preference or future behaviour.
- **Unknown** means the public sources reviewed did not establish it. It is never
  treated as a negative fact.
- **Confidence** measures the directness, recency, and completeness of evidence,
  not scholarly quality.

## Scope limits

No interview, private CV, student survey, thesis registry audit, grant-office
record, internal laboratory record, or raw coating dataset was available. The
coating repository explicitly withholds raw nanoindentation data and directs
requests to Uğurlu/Bal; it does not demonstrate that a new student can access or
publish from those data. [Repository README](https://github.com/yauz3/coating_motifs_ml)

Consequently, claims about mentoring quality, capacity, funding, data rights,
supervision chemistry, or PhD placement are deliberately conditional.
