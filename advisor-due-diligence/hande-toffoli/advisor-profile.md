### What the deep-read papers actually contribute

1. **Methane pyrolysis in liquid metals (2024). Fact — high confidence.** This is not a static-barrier-only paper. It develops a protocol around many AIMD simulations, classifies dissociation events across reaction steps, and links dissociation times to local environment, binding-energy, and Bader-charge descriptors. It compares Cu and Al promoters in Bi–Ni molten alloys and reports Cu as the better promoter under the simulated conditions. The methods specify VASP, PBE, PAW, D3, 340 eV cutoff, NPT/NVT at 3000 K, and Packmol-built alloy cells; the acknowledgments identify TRUBA resources. [Full article](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsaem.3c03235)

2. **Graphene/Au AFM tribology (2024). Fact — high confidence.** This paper separates tip shape from tip deformation instead of treating “the tip” as a fixed black box. It runs finite-temperature LAMMPS simulations using EAM for Au, AIREBO for C–C, a Lennard-Jones Au–C interaction, a 1 fs velocity-Verlet step, and defined NVT preparation/production protocols. Its substantive result is that tip geometry and direction materially change stick–slip/friction, while deformable tips flatten and change contact; a larger tip produced a more experimentally comparable stick–slip signal. [Full article](https://doi.org/10.3390/lubricants12020046)

3. **Pt-doped Au(111) for methanol dehydrogenation (2023). Fact — high confidence.** The work uses open-source Quantum ESPRESSO and XCrySDen with explicit slab and optimization choices. It systematically varies Pt substitution/overlayer/sub-layer models rather than assuming one dopant configuration. It concludes that Pt doping lowers the elementary dehydrogenation barrier, with a particularly low reported barrier when atomic oxygen pretreatment is combined with Pt doping. [Full article](https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules28237928)

4. **Water on boron surfaces (2023). Fact — high confidence.** The study compares reconstructed, unreconstructed, and defective alpha-boron surfaces plus a beta-boron subunit, for one and multiple water molecules. Its contribution is phase-sensitive adsorption analysis: the reported adsorption strengths differ markedly across defect/phase and water multiplicity, rather than collapsing boron into one ideal surface. [Full article](https://doi.org/10.30728/boron.1283831)

5. **DFT/MD polymer–carbon interfaces (2021). Fact — high confidence.** This is a genuine multiscale workflow: Quantum ESPRESSO/vdW-DF1-C09 calculations on PEEK subunits/monomers benchmark a ReaxFF-lg LAMMPS protocol for longer chains on graphene and CNTs. It then examines chain length, temperature, substrate, and starting orientation rather than asserting a generic nanocomposite benefit. The study finds stronger PEEK interaction with graphene than CNTs in the investigated models; its CRediT statement assigns Hande Ustunel supervision, investigation, original-draft writing, review/editing, and project administration. [Primary postprint](https://arts.units.it/bitstream/11368/2992708/5/CMS-2021-TOFFOLI-Post_print.pdf)

6. **h-BN/Au(111) nanotribology (2019). Fact — high confidence.** This is another explicit QE study: it maps an h-BN/Au potential-energy surface, checks convergence tightly, uses vdW-DF2-C09, and compares a periodic interface with Au clusters to isolate edge/size effects. It reports a very low zero-load corrugation for the periodic interface and a friction coefficient decreasing to 0.005 at the highest stated load, while Au clusters show much larger lateral forces. Hande Ustunel is corresponding author. [Full article](https://open.metu.edu.tr/bitstream/handle/11511/28648/Nanotribological_properties_of_the_h_bn_au%28111%29_interface_a_dft_study.pdf)

7. **Formaldehyde selectivity on silver (2021). Fact — high confidence.** This multidisciplinary study combines XPS, LEED, temperature-programmed reaction spectroscopy, infrared spectroscopy, and DFT to distinguish oxygen states on Ag(111). Its result is a mechanism-level selectivity trade-off: low electrophilic-oxygen coverage is linked to formaldehyde selectivity, while higher coverage reconstructs the surface into oxide domains that favor total oxidation. Hande Ustunel is a coauthor; the accessible text does not assign individual roles, so this does not establish her personal experimental/DFT share. [Primary postprint](https://arts.units.it/retrieve/07f53437-61ba-4a31-937a-4b56ebdda8c4/acscatal_2021_TOFFOLI-Post_print.pdf)

8. **DFT tribology review (2022). Fact — high confidence.** This is a review, not evidence of a new experimental result. It is nevertheless useful methodological evidence: it sets out DFT’s role for potential-energy surfaces, shear strength, adsorption, and substrate effects, while reserving velocity/temperature-dependent behavior for classical MD. That separation is directly relevant to a candidate proposing an analysis or workflow tool that must not blur methodological limits. [OpenMETU record and full text](https://open.metu.edu.tr/handle/11511/98251)
