# Final decision — Akdeniz University pilot

## Recommendation

**Recommend Akdeniz University conditionally for a Materials Science and Engineering thesis MSc, not as an unconditional long-term computational-materials destination.** The condition is a confirmed, written project/supervision arrangement centered on **Sadettin Yavuz Uğurlu plus Emre Bal** or an equally evidence-supported alternative. This recommendation rests on the current official MSE MSc listing, the explicit eligibility of Computer Engineering graduates in the 2026–27 call, and the public coatings-ML bridge; it does not rest on reputation. [B09a](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources) [B27](bibliography.md#materials-science-and-engineering--primary-profiles-and-selected-publications)

| Another path | **Preferred if the pair cannot commit** | Seek an established computational-materials group with verified DFT/MD/materials-informatics software, supervisors, and data access. |

## Go / no-go test

Proceed only if all four answers are positive: (1) Uğurlu confirms availability and thesis-supervision role; (2) Bal or another materials-domain partner confirms experimental scope/data; (3) the project permits durable, reproducible software ownership; and (4) a future PhD strategy is clear. If any fail, Akdeniz is still useful as a CSE/data-science option but is not the best evidenced route to AI for materials discovery.

**Confidence: medium.** The sources strongly support the described bridge, but public records cannot verify supervision chemistry, data availability, project continuity, or future admissions decisions.
