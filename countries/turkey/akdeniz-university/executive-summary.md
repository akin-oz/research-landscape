# Akdeniz University pilot — executive summary

**Decision question:** Which currently listed staff members are the best evidence-based long-term advisor fits for a senior software engineer moving into scientific software, computational materials science, and AI for materials discovery?

**Bottom line:** The public record supports a **two-person, cross-department strategy**, not a single ready-made “AI-for-materials” supervisor. On the Computer Engineering (CSE) side, **Hüseyin Gökhan Akçay**, **Taner Danışman**, and **Melih Günay** have the clearest public evidence for ML/vision, parallel or distributed computing, and data-oriented methods. On the Materials side, **Meltem Asiltürk Ersoy**, **Emre Bal**, and **Numan Hoda** have the clearest evidence of active materials research. No reviewed source establishes a dedicated local DFT, molecular-dynamics, CALPHAD, materials-informatics, or AI-for-materials research line in either target department. ## What is evidenced

- CSE has publicly described ML/deep-learning activity and a university AI/Data Science Center that reports GPU/HPC-capable environments and AI software-development support. This supports feasibility for computational work, but it does not establish individual supervision of materials AI. [B06](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources) [B07](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources)
- CSE has evidenced MSc and PhD pathways; university listings show an MSE MSc pathway, while a Materials PhD should be verified directly before relying on it. [B03](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources) [B04](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources) [B05](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources)
- Materials faculty profiles show experimental nanocomposites, coatings, polymer/nanoparticle work, sol-gel/photovoltaics, and thermal-barrier materials. These are genuine materials-science entry points but are not evidence of a computational-materials software culture.
