## Suggested first email shape

State the actual bridge: “I am a senior software engineer moving into reproducible scientific software and materials ML. I reviewed the public coatings-ML work and would like to discuss a scoped MSc project on provenance, leakage-controlled modelling, uncertainty, and experimental validation.” Link a short GitHub/Materials Atlas portfolio, then ask one concrete availability question. Do not claim familiarity with tools or data that the public record does not establish.

## Questions to ask every candidate

- Are you accepting an MSc student in the next intake, and can you be principal supervisor or co-supervisor?
- Is there an active, thesis-appropriate dataset and a clear publication/data-rights route?
- What coding, version-control, reproducibility, and compute practices do thesis students actually use?
- Can a candidate own an end-to-end software artifact alongside a defensible materials question?

## Topics to avoid

- Generic “AI will solve materials discovery” claims.
- Assuming DFT/MD, ASE/pymatgen, GPU access, data openness, or supervision capacity without confirmation.
- Treating this compatibility analysis as a reputational ranking.

The contact sequence is grounded in the dossier evidence and should be revised immediately if a candidate's capacity, current role, or project status differs from public records.
