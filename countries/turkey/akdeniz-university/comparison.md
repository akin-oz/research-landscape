## Interpretation

For the stated goal, **MSE MSc with a Uğurlu–Bal thesis structure** is the best evidence-based local route: it puts materials questions and a proven coating-ML bridge at the centre. **CSE MSc** is the better choice only if the candidate's real priority is scientific/software infrastructure or general ML, and a materials problem can be formally co-supervised. A one-department choice without a named cross-disciplinary project is materially weaker in either direction. [Uğurlu dossier](materials-engineering/faculty/sadettin-yavuz-ugurlu.md) · [Bal dossier](materials-engineering/faculty/emre-bal.md) · [CSE curriculum](bibliography.md#institutional-and-department-sources)

## Evidence gaps to resolve before applying

1. Who can formally supervise/co-supervise the proposed thesis, and who has capacity?
2. Is the coating ML repository active, publishable, and supported by accessible data?
3. What computer/GPU access, code-review expectations, and data governance apply to thesis students?
4. Is there a written MSc-to-PhD pathway if the candidate later needs a computational-materials PhD outside MSE?
