# Top departments and programmes for the target profile

1. **ITU Informatics Institute — Computational Science and Engineering:** strongest direct blend of materials informatics, HPC, scripting/scientific software, and crystal-structure prediction through the Tekin environment. [N01](nationwide-bibliography.md)
2. **Koç Chemical and Biological Engineering / NEMO:** strongest direct MOF-data/ML/open-research environment. [N08](nationwide-bibliography.md)
3. **METU Physics:** strongest evidence for a hands-on DFT/MD computational-materials foundation. [N02](nationwide-bibliography.md)
4. **Bilkent Materials Science and Nanotechnology (UNAM/MSN):** strong computational-materials cluster and visible graduate/alumni infrastructure. [N11](nationwide-bibliography.md) [N12](nationwide-bibliography.md)
5. **Sabancı Materials Science and Nano Engineering:** direct computational energy-materials projects and cross-disciplinary MSc projects. [N10](nationwide-bibliography.md)
6. **ITU Metallurgical and Materials Engineering:** best materials-first route among the shortlist for experimentally grounded DFT/CALPHAD modelling. [N06](nationwide-bibliography.md)

This list is deliberately not a university-quality ranking. The best department is the one whose named supervisor will commit to a software-owning, materials-valid thesis.

## Screened but not promoted

Gazi, Ege, Dokuz Eylül, and Marmara were reviewed only at triage level. The search did not find a current faculty environment with an evidence bundle comparable to the shortlisted direct matches—namely clear computational-materials relevance plus either research software, reproducible workflow, materials-ML, or strong scientific-computing evidence. This is an evidence-gap decision for this candidate profile, not a judgment of institutional quality. Boğaziçi, Hacettepe, GTU, YTU, IZTECH, and Ankara University were retained because at least one current candidate crossed that threshold.
