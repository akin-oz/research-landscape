# Career roadmap: software engineering → computational materials research

**Applicant context:** 10+ years senior software engineering; current work on Materials Atlas and Research Landscape; learning Scientific Python, NumPy, SciPy, ASE, pymatgen and computational materials science.

## Recommended direction

Build a public research-software identity around a narrow, technically real problem—not a portfolio of disconnected notebooks. A high-value sequence is:

1. **Reproduce one published computational workflow** using ASE + pymatgen, with pinned environments, tests, a data provenance record and an honest methods note.
2. **Contribute upstream** to one ecosystem (first choice: pymatgen/atomate2, ASE, AiiDA, NOMAD or Materials Project tooling). Begin with docs, tests, bug reproduction or API examples; graduate to a scoped feature only after maintainer feedback.
3. **Build Materials Atlas as a research object.** Give it a declared data model, provenance, licences, versioned datasets, benchmark tasks and a reproducibility policy. Avoid scraping or redistributing data without the source licence.
4. **Use Research Landscape as a methodological companion.** Its evidence/confidence model is an unusual differentiator if it remains factual, versioned and privacy-respecting.
5. **Earn scientific credibility through a small collaboration.** Aim for a reproducible benchmark, workflow or dataset paper/conference poster before trying to lead a broad materials-AI project.

## Destinations by career stage

| Stage | Highest-fit environments | Why | Entry proof that matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| MSc | A lab with a defined computational project and daily code review/mentoring; a programme connected to AiiDA/Materials Cloud, Materials Project, OQMD, AFLOW, ASE/GPAW, NIMS or RIKEN. | Converts engineering experience into domain-method competence. | A reproducible mini-project, Python package PRs, DFT/MD literacy and clear research questions. |
| PhD | A software-producing computational materials lab with access to theory mentors and experimental collaborators. | Gives a publishable scientific question plus durable software/community impact. | First poster/preprint, sustained upstream contribution, and evidence you can debug scientific—not just web—software. |
| Postdoc | A cross-institutional infrastructure or methods group with ownership of an open platform. | Supports leadership in workflow, data, ML-potential or FAIR research infrastructure. | Lead-maintainer experience, release/review record, first-author methods/data paper and collaborator references. |

## Communities and venues

- **Software/data:** Materials Project, AiiDA, Materials Cloud, NOMAD/FAIRmat, ASE, pymatgen, matminer, OpenKIM, LAMMPS, Quantum ESPRESSO, ABINIT, DeepModeling and MACE. See [source register](global-sources.md).
- **Research meetings:** MRS meetings, TMS, the Psi-k community, CECAM workshops, PASC, SciPy, MolSSI workshops, the Materials Research Society data/AI sessions, NOMAD/MARVEL/Materials Cloud schools when offered.
- **Conference strategy:** submit a reproducibility or infrastructure poster early; use it to request 15-minute feedback conversations, not a job. Record questions and follow up with a concrete artifact.

## 12-month evidence plan

| Quarter | Outcome | Quality bar |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Q1 | One fully reproducible ASE/pymatgen study | README, environment lock, testable code, input/output provenance and limitations. |
| Q2 | One merged upstream contribution | Small, reviewed, covered by tests where appropriate; no vanity PRs. |
| Q3 | Materials Atlas v0.1 | Clear scope, source licences, schema, CI, citation guidance and one useful query/visualization. |
| Q4 | Public research artifact | Poster, workshop talk, preprint, benchmark or a collaborator-endorsed technical report. |

## Contact timing

Contact an anchor lab **after** you can reference one relevant artifact or a precise question about their current work. An exception is a listed MSc/PhD opening with a deadline: apply/ask immediately, then attach the project-in-progress. The best first channel is usually a concise, tailored email; GitHub issue/PR discussion is appropriate only for the project itself. Never use an issue tracker as an admissions channel.
