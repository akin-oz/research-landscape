# Global research ecosystems for computational materials and scientific software

**Decision lens:** fit for a senior software engineer moving into computational materials science—not national prestige.  
**Evidence window:** sources reviewed 2026-07-11; see [source register](global-sources.md).  
**Important:** an ecosystem is a connected set of laboratories, data/software infrastructure, compute access, funders and collaborators. A country may contain several very different ecosystems.

## Method

Ratings are **opportunity signals**, not ranks: `strong`, `present`, `unclear`. They describe publicly visible infrastructure, not an applicant's likelihood of admission. Mentorship, current funding, language requirements, visa eligibility and openings are intentionally not scored without lab- and programme-specific evidence.

## Interpretation for this candidate

The best **first targets** are the ecosystems where software is a research output: Berkeley–DOE/Materials Project, Swiss AiiDA/Materials Cloud, NOMAD/FAIRmat, AFLOW, OQMD/CHiMaD, Nordic ASE/GPAW and selected NIMS/RIKEN teams. They provide concrete contribution surfaces—documentation, parsers, APIs, workflow engines, tests, schemas and benchmarks—where a senior engineer can show value before formal admission.

The strongest **scientific fit** is not automatically the strongest **mentoring fit**. Before applying, compare two to four groups using the checklist in [the PI index](top-100-principal-investigators.md#due-diligence-before-contact). Treat current openings, funding, team composition, graduate outcomes, work-life norms and immigration as required local checks.

## Funding, PhD structure, language, work-life and immigration: evidence boundary

| Dimension requested | What the current evidence supports | What this report deliberately does not claim |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Funding | Public programme/institute evidence exists for Berkeley–DOE/Materials Project (S01), MARVEL (S08), FAIRmat through 2026 (S35), RIKEN (S15–S17), NIMS (S13) and CAS materials AI (S18). | That a particular student is funded, a grant is available to a named PI, or one ecosystem is “better funded.” |
| PhD structure | The reports identify research infrastructures and prospective supervising environments. | Cross-national duration, stipend, coursework or supervision rules; those are programme-specific and change. |
| Research-software and open-source culture | Directly assessed where code, docs, APIs, workflow/provenance platforms or licences are public. | That all groups in the same country or university share that culture. |
| Work-life balance | No score: public research pages are not reliable evidence. | A country- or lab-wide work-life judgment. Ask current trainees privately and aggregate only consented observations. |
| Language barriers | Some sources are English-language and some Chinese/Japanese; this establishes documentation accessibility only. | Working-language, thesis-language or classroom requirements. Verify the exact programme and team. |
| Immigration | No score: eligibility depends on citizenship, degree type, funding/employment contract and current law. | A general claim that any country is easy/hard to enter or stay in. Check the official immigration authority and institution’s international office at application time. |

This restraint is intentional. A durable knowledge base should record the research evidence now and attach dated, primary programme/legal evidence only when a specific application path is in scope.
