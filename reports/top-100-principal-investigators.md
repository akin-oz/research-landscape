## Due diligence before contact

Every candidate needs the same evidence review. A strong publication record or a famous codebase is **not** evidence of mentoring quality.

| Question | What counts as evidence | Do not infer from |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Is this person currently an eligible supervisor? | Current institutional directory + graduate-programme rules. | Search snippets, old CVs or project history. |
| Is there a funded project/opening? | Current vacancy or funder/project record. | A large group or recent papers. |
| Does the lab value software engineering? | Maintained public repos, issue/PR process, docs, tests/releases, named software roles. | A GitHub badge or a single archived repository. |
| Is mentorship likely to fit? | Student/alumni conversations, coauthorship patterns, handbook, meeting expectations, transparent outcomes. | Citation count, awards, country or lab size. |
| Does the project fit Materials Atlas/Research Landscape? | A specific intersection with their workflow/data/reproducibility problem. | A generic “AI for materials” label. |

Promote a `Discovery` entry to a full dossier only after adding (1) current role and supervision eligibility, (2) one direct research source, (3) one software/open-science source where applicable, (4) current group/position evidence, and (5) a dated contact hypothesis.
