# Computational Phonon Calculations

Computational Phonon Calculations is a controlled research area for entities
whose own reviewed evidence explicitly describes phonon calculations used to
compute material properties. It creates a topic-first discovery route without
equating every atomistic simulation, vibrational method, or materials project.

## Evidence

| Source ID | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| `SRC-PHONOPY-DOCUMENTATION` | [Phonopy documentation](https://phonopy.github.io/phonopy/) describes Phonopy as an open-source package for harmonic and quasi-harmonic phonon calculations and documents phonon band structures, thermal properties, and quasi-harmonic thermal expansion. Accessed 2026-07-13. |

## Boundary and limitations

The label is used only when the entity's own evidence explicitly describes
computational phonon calculations. It does not rank research problems, methods,
software, groups, advisors, or institutions, prescribe a workflow, or classify
every material property, atomistic simulation, or phonon-adjacent activity.
