# Machine-Learned Potentials for Materials

Machine-Learned Potentials for Materials is a controlled research area for
machine-learned potential models explicitly described for atomistic simulation
or materials applications. It provides a precise discovery path within AI for
Materials without treating every machine-learning model, force field,
atomistic workflow, or data-driven materials project as equivalent.

## Evidence

| Source ID | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| `SRC-FAIRCHEM-INTRODUCTION` | [FAIR Chemistry: GNNs for Chemistry](https://fair-chem.github.io/intro/) describes machine-learned potentials as surrogate models for DFT and describes graph-based models for atomistic systems; the documentation identifies their applications from catalysis to materials properties. Accessed 2026-07-12. |

## Boundary and limitations

This label is used only when an entity's own evidence explicitly describes a
machine-learned potential and its materials or atomistic-simulation scope. It
does not rank models, assert comparable accuracy, treat a potential as a
universal replacement for DFT, or classify every AI-for-Materials activity.
