# Density-Functional Theory and Electronic Structure

Density-Functional Theory and Electronic Structure is a controlled discovery
area for software whose own reviewed source explicitly describes
electronic-structure calculations or DFT-based electronic-structure modelling
for materials, molecules, or periodic systems. It creates a narrower traversal
than Computational Materials Science without defining a universal hierarchy of
theory, chemistry, or materials subjects.

## Evidence

| Source ID | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| `SRC-QE-HOME` | [Quantum ESPRESSO](https://www.quantum-espresso.org/) describes open-source codes for electronic-structure calculations and nanoscale materials modelling based on density-functional theory, plane waves, and pseudopotentials. Accessed 2026-07-13. |
| `SRC-ABINIT-PRESENTATION` | [ABINIT: Presentation](https://www.abinit.org/presentation.html) describes DFT electronic-structure calculations for molecules and periodic solids using pseudopotentials or PAW data and a plane-wave basis. Accessed 2026-07-13. |
| `SRC-CP2K-REPOSITORY` | [cp2k/cp2k](https://github.com/cp2k/cp2k) describes DFT methods, including GPW and GAPW, within its quantum-chemistry and solid-state-physics atomistic-simulation package. Accessed 2026-07-13. |

## Boundary and limitations

This label is applied only where an entity's own reviewed source explicitly
describes DFT or electronic-structure calculations in the documented scope. It
does not classify every computational-materials project, force field,
machine-learned potential, workflow, dataset, or group; it makes no claim about
method appropriateness, accuracy, performance, research quality, or fit.
