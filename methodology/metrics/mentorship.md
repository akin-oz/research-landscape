# Mentorship-process evidence

Mentorship is consequential but not reliably observable from reputation,
awards, group size, citation counts, a lab photograph, or an isolated
testimonial. Research Landscape therefore treats it first as a narrow,
time-bounded **public process-evidence** question—not a quality score,
personality assessment, capacity signal, or prediction of an applicant's
experience.

## What may be collected

Collect only public, aggregate, professionally relevant statements that name a
specific practice and source. Eligible categories include:

| Category | Examples of bounded evidence | Does not establish |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Written expectations | Public handbook, code of conduct, advising compact, meeting/feedback expectations | That the practice is universal or enforced. |
| Onboarding and training | Public onboarding guide, documented training curriculum, safety/computing orientation | Individual support quality or an available place. |
| Supervision process | Publicly stated advising structure, committee/process guidance, recurring progress-review practice | A particular advisor's behavior or capacity. |
| Professional development | Public writing, presentation, career-development, or responsible-research training | A career outcome or placement guarantee. |
| Aggregate outcomes | Public, contextual, aggregate programme outcomes with denominator, period, and method | Causality, an individual outcome, or comparability across unlike populations. |

Each observation must identify its exact entity scope, source URL/identifier,
access date, relevant wording or value, time scope, and limitation. A group
handbook may support a group-process observation; it must not be silently
promoted to a university-wide claim. A programme-wide policy must not be
attributed to every research group.

## What must not be collected or scored

- private communications, unpublished complaints, rumors, or anonymous claims;
- sensitive personal data, protected characteristics, health, immigration,
  financial status, or interpersonal conflict;
- individual student trajectories unless the person has made the professional
  information public and its collection is necessary and proportionate;
- uncontextualized alumni names, awards, degree completions, social-media
  endorsements, rankings, or review-site sentiment;
- an aggregate “mentorship quality,” “best mentor,” or admissions-likelihood
  score.

Absence of a public process document is unknown evidence, not a negative
finding. A documented process is a discovery lead for a prospective researcher
to discuss directly with the programme or potential advisor; it is not a
promise.

## Review and comparability gate

Before a public discovery query can surface a group or programme, the proposed
evidence contract must require, at minimum:

1. a controlled evidence category and a source-specific scope;
2. a current or explicitly historical time boundary;
3. source and editorial limitations shown beside the result; and
4. enough reviewed coverage to make a query useful without presenting a sparse
   cohort as comparative performance.

Different kinds of evidence are not numerically interchangeable. A public
handbook and an aggregate outcome report can be displayed as separate,
explainable signals, but they must not be added into a universal mentorship
score. Any future use in a private fit model requires a declared applicant
preference, an explicit missing-data rule, and human judgment.

## Current status

The v2 graph has no approved structured field or predicate for these
observations. Until [ADR 0008](../../docs/adr/0008-mentorship-process-evidence-contract.md)
is approved and demonstrated by small reviewed vertical slices, retain bounded
process evidence in a record's narrative and keep mentorship recommendations
unavailable.
