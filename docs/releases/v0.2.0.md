# Research Landscape v0.2.0 — Research Relationship Management

This release adds Research Relationship Management (RRM): a Markdown-first, evidence-driven system for turning careful advisor research into respectful, long-term academic relationship practice.

## Highlights

- RRM lifecycle from discovery and research through first contact, meetings, follow-up, application, admission, and long-term mentorship.
- Reusable, automation-ready Markdown templates for timelines, contacts, meetings, notes, actions, reading, research ideas, applications, and research-focused emails.
- Explicit relationship philosophy: research before admissions, curiosity before requests, evidence before assumptions, and long-term trust over short-term outcomes.
- Privacy and scope boundaries that make RRM neither a CRM nor a networking database.
- Three prepared, uncontacted relationship records linked to the existing due-diligence dossiers for Adem Tekin, Sadettin Yavuz Uğurlu, and Hande Toffoli.
- Deep due-diligence dossiers and final comparison for the narrowed three-advisor shortlist.

## What RRM deliberately does not do

RRM does not collect private contact data, infer relationship quality from response times, send messages, schedule reminders, rank people by responsiveness, or convert private interactions into public research evidence.

The prepared advisor records contain no invented emails, meetings, replies, outcomes, applications, or mentorship claims. They are structured starting points for an applicant’s own factual records.

## Next milestone

The next release will focus on an Automation Framework: reproducible validation, source-ingestion safeguards, score-generation controls, and freshness monitoring. Any automation will preserve the RRM boundary between mechanical validation and human relationship judgment.
