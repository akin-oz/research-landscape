# Methodology

## Collection

Collect only information relevant to research environment and applicant fit. Capture the original source, access date, scope, extraction method, and evidence window. Prefer sources that describe the entity directly and archive permissible snapshots or identifiers where practical.

## Acceptable sources

Primary sources include official institutional, lab, funding-agency, repository, publisher, and researcher-maintained pages. Structured scholarly indexes may support discovery or aggregation but should be checked against primary records for consequential claims. Reputable secondary reporting is allowed only when primary evidence is unavailable and is labeled accordingly.

## Citations and confidence

Every factual claim has an inline link or evidence-table citation. Confidence is **high** for recent, direct, corroborated evidence; **medium** for direct but incomplete or older evidence; **low** for indirect, uncorroborated, or limited-scope evidence. Confidence measures evidence quality, not entity quality.

## Missing and conflicting information

Use `not available` or `insufficient evidence`; do not substitute zero. Explain coverage limits. Preserve material conflicts, cite both sources, and avoid a score until the conflict is resolved or the calculation explicitly supports uncertainty.

## Conflicts and reproducibility

Contributors disclose institutional, financial, supervisory, or personal relationships that could affect a report. A reviewer with a material conflict should not be sole approver. Reports record model version, retrieval window, sources, calculations, and revisions so another contributor can reproduce the result.
