# Follow-up strategy

Follow-up is an act of clarity and respect, not escalation. A good follow-up makes it easy for an advisor to decline, defer, or give one useful direction without pressure.

## Expected response windows

| Situation | Default wait before one follow-up | Exception |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First research-advice email | 10–14 calendar days | Wait longer around a stated leave period, deadline, or holiday. |
| A promised item after a meeting | Send on the agreed date; otherwise within 24 hours of the meeting | Do not send material the advisor did not request. |
| A question tied to a live paper or research task | 7–10 business days | If an explicit response date was given, use that date instead. |
| Formal application update | Only after a material, truthful update or when the programme process invites it | Never use an update as a disguised admissions-demand email. |

## When to follow up

Follow up once when all of these are true:

1. The first message was concise, personalized, and research-focused.
2. The wait window has passed.
3. There is one clear reason to reopen the thread: a question, requested material, a completed preparation task, or a timely research update.
4. The next action is small and respectful of the advisor’s time.

Record the message and its status in the advisor’s `contact-log.md` before sending it. Update `action-items.md` with the next review date rather than relying on memory.

## When not to follow up

Do not follow up:

- before the agreed or default wait window;
- repeatedly after one unanswered follow-up;
- through unrelated channels after email silence;
- to request an admissions guarantee, funding promise, or future reference;
- when the advisor has declined, asked not to be contacted, or referred the applicant to a formal process;
- with a large attachment, generic progress report, or a newly invented urgency.

Silence is not evidence about an advisor’s quality or intent. It is simply unresolved information and should be logged as such.

## Professional form

A follow-up should contain four elements: a short reminder of the research-specific context, one truthful update or question, a low-pressure request, and permission not to reply. Keep it shorter than the original note. The reusable email template should be tailored only after the applicant has reviewed the advisor’s latest public work and the prior correspondence.

## Graceful stop rule

After one unanswered follow-up, set the relationship record to `closed` with the neutral closure reason `no-response-after-one-follow-up`, record the dated decision in the timeline, set no further automatic action, and continue the broader research plan. If a later reply creates a new stated research purpose, record a dated reactivation decision before resuming. Do not make a negative inference or write a subjective judgment.

## Long-term relationship rule

After an application or admission outcome, interaction should still be useful to both sides: share only work that is relevant, thank people precisely, honour stated boundaries, and avoid treating a person as a route to credentials. A relationship record should make that discipline visible over time.
